Teens have the right to free expression

Bill C-34 cuts young Canadians off from the digital world under the guise of protection. Join BackOnline, the youth-led movement defending the Charter right of minors to connect, express themselves, and access information online. Contact your MP today.

A Law That Doesn't Add Up.

Requiring everyone — adults included — to verify their identity before using social media to address concerns about minors is a blunt, privacy-invasive solution to a narrow problem.

Section 2(b) of the Charter protects freedom of expression, including the ability to access information and communicate anonymously, and a blanket verification mandate risks chilling that speech for millions while creating a tempting new database of sensitive personal data — without solving the underlying issue.

Get prepared

Information will appear here as needed, there are currently no restrictions for under 16's going on social media in Canada. (Updated June 15, 2026)

While we're uncertain, you can already prepare by downloading 4 apps on your iPhone.

  • Proton VPN: A secure, zero-logs virtual private network service offering reliable free tier servers.
  • Onion Browser: An open-source browser utility utilizing localized routing technology to maximize online privacy.
  • WireGuard: A fast, modern protocol that allows users to configure custom, private VPN connections manually.
  • Mullvad VPN: A privacy-focused, paid VPN service heavily optimized for censorship resistance and anonymity.
